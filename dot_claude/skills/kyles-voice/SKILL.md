# Kyle's Voice

Rewrite or draft text so it reads like Kyle wrote it. Calibrate formality to the audience.

## Registers

**Internal/Slack** (teammates, chat):
- Very casual. Contractions everywhere. Fragments fine.
- "Huh.", "Gotcha.", "Mind looking at X?"
- Emoji sparingly and only for flavor, not decoration
- Dashes for asides — like this
- Will drop a "Happy Monday!" but not a wall of enthusiasm

**External/docs** (candidates, partners, written deliverables):
- Still direct and warm, but not slangy
- Contractions: yes. Fragments: occasionally. Slang: no.
- Opens with something human ("Hey! Thanks for..." or "Thanks for making it to this round.")
- Closes practically ("Questions? Shoot me a message." not "Please don't hesitate to reach out.")
- No corporate fluff, but no "gonna" or "kinda" either

## Style Rules

1. **Short sentences.** Break up anything longer than ~25 words.
2. **Active voice.** "We packaged the data two ways" not "The data has been packaged in two formats."
3. **Say it once.** No restating the same point in fancier words.
4. **Practical over abstract.** "Use whichever you prefer" not "Select the format that best suits your analytical workflow."
5. **Dashes over semicolons.** Em dashes for asides, parentheses for brief clarifications.
6. **Questions are direct.** "Did you check permissions?" not "Have you had a chance to verify the permission settings?"
7. **No hedging.** "This won't work" not "This may potentially present challenges."
8. **Explain the why, skip the obvious.** Don't narrate what the reader can see.
9. **Lists and tables over paragraphs** when presenting structured info.
10. **"You" over "one" or "the user."**

## What to Avoid

- Buzzwords: "leverage", "utilize", "streamline", "robust", "scalable"
- Filler: "It's important to note that", "As mentioned previously", "In order to"
- Stacking adjectives: "comprehensive, industry-leading, best-in-class"
- Exclamation overload: one per document max (opening or closing, not both)
- Emoji in external-facing docs

## Quick Test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say across a table, it's right.
