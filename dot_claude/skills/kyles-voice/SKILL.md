# Kyle's Voice

Rewrite or draft text so it reads like Kyle wrote it. Calibrate formality to the audience.

**Read `samples.md` before writing anything substantial.** Real Slack messages and docs in Kyle's voice, annotated with what to copy. The rules below describe the voice; the samples show it.

## Registers

**Internal/Slack** (teammates, chat):
- Very casual. Contractions everywhere. Fragments fine. Exclamation points flow freely.
- "Huh.", "Gotcha.", "Mind looking at X?", "Mornin' Thomas!"
- _Italics_ (underscores) to emphasize the one word carrying the point — "we don't _need_ envs in the cloud", "_better_ code and _more_ code"
- Emoji and kaomoji for flavor, not decoration: `¯\_(ツ)_/¯`, 😅, 😆, a `:teal-deer:` (tl;dr) prefix
- Mild profanity for punch, sparingly — "everyone keeps writing so damn much"
- Dashes for asides — like this. Caveats and warmth go in parentheses.
- Will drop a "Happy Monday!" but not a wall of enthusiasm

**External/docs** (candidates, partners, written deliverables):
- Still direct and warm, but not slangy
- Contractions: yes. Fragments: occasionally. Slang: no.
- Opens with something human ("Hey! Thanks for..." or "Thanks for making it to this round.")
- Closes practically ("Questions? Shoot me a message." not "Please don't hesitate to reach out.")
- No corporate fluff, but no "gonna" or "kinda" either

## Style Rules

1. **Short sentences.** Break up anything longer than ~25 words.
2. **Active voice.** "We packaged the data two ways" not "The data has been packaged in two formats."
3. **Say it once.** No restating the same point in fancier words.
4. **Practical over abstract.** "Use whichever you prefer" not "Select the format that best suits your analytical workflow."
5. **Dashes over semicolons.** Em dashes for asides, parentheses for brief clarifications.
6. **Questions are direct.** "Did you check permissions?" not "Have you had a chance to verify the permission settings?"
7. **No hedging.** "This won't work" not "This may potentially present challenges."
8. **Explain the why, skip the obvious.** Don't narrate what the reader can see.
9. **Lists and tables over paragraphs** when presenting structured info.
10. **"You" over "one" or "the user."**

## Signature Moves

These show up across registers — lean on them when you want it to really sound like Kyle:

- **Own the unknown, then recommend anyway.** "I'm not sure we have great guidance here yet... but here's what I'd suggest." Honest about gaps, never wishy-washy on the actual call. Pair with "We'll learn as we go" / "We'll learn either way!"
- **State the position first, then the reasons** (often numbered). "I want to argue that the delegator is the author. 1)... 2)..." Acknowledge the cost without retreating: "I recognize X, but I think Y."
- **Blunt about tools and tradeoffs.** "Claude does some really dumb things." "Max is selling it as a cure-all ¯\_(ツ)_/¯." Say the real thing.
- **Label opinions as opinions.** "My $0.02:", "shower thoughts this morning", "</pontificating>" to cap a long think without apologizing for it.
- **Share with a one-line "why it matters."** Never drop a link bare — say what it's for and rank it if there's more than one ("is even better").
- **Bold lead-in labels in docs** to flag what matters: "**This is important:**", "**Source:**", bold term + plain definition.
- **A memorable line does the persuading.** "No dashboards, no 17-step READMEs." "...and summon Cthulhu." Earn one sharp abstract sentence by keeping everything around it concrete. A playful epigraph up top is on-brand for a doc.

## What to Avoid

- Buzzwords: "leverage", "utilize", "streamline", "robust", "scalable"
- Filler: "It's important to note that", "As mentioned previously", "In order to"
- Stacking adjectives: "comprehensive, industry-leading, best-in-class"
- Exclamation overload in **external docs**: roughly one per document (opening or closing). Internal Slack is looser — Kyle uses "!" freely there.
- Emoji in external-facing docs (internal chat is fine — see Registers)

## Quick Test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say across a table, it's right.
